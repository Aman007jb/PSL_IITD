Plasma Simulation Lab

We at the Plasma Theory and Simulations Lab delve into understanding the fundamentals of plasma physics via theory and simulation methods. We are involved in exploring the laser-plasma interactions using the famous 'Particle-In-Cell (PIC) simulations, understanding the complex dusty plasmas and their dynamics using the Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations, and recently we have taken an interest in looking into the high-energy plasmas Yang-Mills plasma or Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) to name a few.

Laser/EM wave interaction with magnetized plasma

Recent technological advancements in laser systems have significantly improved various aspects of laser technology, including the power of petawatt (PW) (also there are proposals for exawatt (EW) power using chirped pulse amplification (CPA) technique), repetition rate, pulse width (a single laser shot can deliver energy of kJ orders in f s timescale), and operational frequency. Laser-plasma interaction is a rapidly growing field of research with significant implications for numerous scientific and diagnostic applications such as the generation of GeV electrons, 100 M eV protons, gamma- ray and X-ray sources, etc. The interaction between a high-intensity laser beam and plasma can result in a variety of complex phenomena, including the generation of high-energy particles, novel radiation source, magnetic field generation and transport. It also holds the potential to aid in laser fusion.
Magnetized plasma opens up the prospects of a new regime in which the electron gyro-frequency can be higher than the laser frequency. In this scenario, one can directly transfer the laser energy to ions. The application of a strong magnetic field also enables low-frequency electromagnetic waves to interact with the bulk overdense plasma through the various permitted passbands of the magnetized dispersion relation. Thus, the laser interaction with magnetized plasma opens the possibility of investigating fundamental aspects of bulk plasma studies. This also provides a wide range of applications for understanding space plasma observations generating collision-less shocks, gamma ray generation, THz generation, magnetic re-connection, fusion studies in strongly magnetized plasma, mechanisms for novel ion acceleration, etc.
